Theotetical Framework

Aleatoric Uncertainty vs. Epistemic Void

Traditional Physical Protection Systems (PPS) like EASI calculate aleatoric uncertainty on known adversary paths. FRAM addresses epistemic uncertainty—the risk of unanalyzed spaces.

Why Fractal Geometry?

Facilities are not Euclidean; they are chaotic. Fractal dimension measures the roughness that hinders responses, and lacunarity measures the texture of sensor blind spots.

Detailed Parameter Classification & Scientific Range
Param. Description & Methodology Range Impact on Total Risk (Rf)
γ Epistemic Uncertainty. Proportion of non-analyzed paths. Determined by facility scale. [0, 1] Multiplier of spatial penalties.
D_f Fractal Dimension of barriers. Extracted via Box-Counting on CAD/GIS floorplans. [1.0, 2.0] High values cause high delay/chaos for response.
Lnorm Normalized Lacunarity of sensor coverage. Extracted via Gliding-Box. [0.0, 1.0] High values mean clustered blind spots (safe attacker transit).
w1 Calibration weight (relative importance of barriers vs sensors). Set by AHP method. [0, 1] Higher w1 means bariers are more critical than sensors.
