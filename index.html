Extended Methodology

Why Normalization?

To ensure mathematical stability and avoid "Double Counting" (multicollinearity), all spatial inputs are normalized to the [0, 1] interval. This ensures that the base PPS risk (Aleatoric) remains primary, while fractal penalties (Epistemic) act as scientific modifiers.

Box-Counting & Gliding-Box

Traditional models treat space as Euclidean. FRAM uses **Box-Counting** to measure fractal dimension (Df), quantifying the architectural chaos. **Gliding-Box** is used for Lacunarity (L), quantifying the "texture" of sensor blind spots.
