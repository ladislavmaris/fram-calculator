Methodology & Parameter Logic

Aleatoric vs Epistemic

Traditional PPS models (EASI) focus on aleatoric uncertainty (probability on a known path). FRAM bridges the epistemic void—the risk of the "unknown" space within a facility.

Why Normalization?

To avoid multicollinearity (double-counting), all inputs are scaled to [0,1]. This ensures the model remains stable across different facility sizes.

Fractal Algorithms

Box-Counting: Used to extract Df (complexity). Measures how pre-defined barriers "fill" the 2D plane.
Gliding-Box: Used to extract L (Lacunarity). Measures the "texture" of empty sensor zones.

Weight Calibration (w1, w2)

Weights allow for expert adjustment (e.g., via AHP) to prioritize either structural barrier complexity or sensor gap distribution.
