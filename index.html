Methodology & Parameter Logic

From PPS to Fractal Risk (FRAM)

Conventional models calculate aleatoric uncertainty (probability on a known path). FRAM bridges the epistemic void—the risk of unknown space within a facility using non-Euclidean geometry.

Box-Counting Method (Df)

Df measures how barriers fill the 2D plane. We use a grid ε to count occupied cells N(ε). Scale [1.0 - 2.0] allows for a linear penalty transition: 1.0 (empty) to 2.0 (total chaos).

Gliding-Box Lacunarity (L)

Lacunarity (L) quantifies the 'texture' of blind spots. It identifies if gaps are uniform or clustered. Normalization to [0,1] enables integration into the risk equation without scale bias.

Normalization & AHP Weights (w1, w2)

Weights w1 (barriers) and w2 (sensors) are complementary (w1 + w2 = 1). They are determined by the AHP method (Analytic Hierarchy Process) to adjust for specific facility types.
